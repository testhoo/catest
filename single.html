The number of unemployed people in Russia is growing rapidly (the most difficult part is yet to come)

  • Laverne
  • 12.09.2021

Now there are already 30 million unemployed people in our country, according to statistics.The crisis is the result of an unfavorable mix of unfavorable circumstances. The coronavirus, which came to our country almost simultaneously with the collapse in oil prices, had nothing to do with it. Yes, unemployment is rising in our country. But we have a good reason for this — the extremely high cost of renting housing and real estate. The fact is that work for yourself — whether you're a good specialist or an extremely mediocre one — is very expensive now. This is because the cost of renting housing and real estate has soared. Russians are suffering from a new financial crisis. This is a unique situation, in which it is not housing and apartment prices that are to blame for the crisis, but a serious decline in income tax receipts by the state. Here's what the monthly housing/land tax bill for September showed:1) the Negative impact of the crisis is still not over yetIMPORTANT! This is temporary. The decline in income tax receipts will continue until long-term financial conditions improve. 2) the number of former Soviet citizens who are still officially employed in the state housing and municipal services is growing rapidly (it increased by 30 thousand people in September). This is a record number of former Soviet citizens who have become unemployed in the current crisis. 3) the cost of housing and communal services continues to grow This crisis has hit all of us at once.The demand for housing and communal services has decreased. This has hit everyone.Property prices are falling. Rentals continue to rise. Due to the slump in the economy, the cost of loans for a new car or apartment has increased. This is all the more reason to buy shares and other financial instruments.4) the Cost of loans for a new car or apartment is FallingToday, many of us are using a car or apartment for the first time.And this is not a joke at all! We are all going to have to change our financial habits.We were once again encouraged by the coronavirus to "live within our means".
